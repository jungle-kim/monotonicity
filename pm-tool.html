Personal workplace dashboard

Feb 2026

I have two problems: I have tried various iterations of to do lists, with pen and paper being the best, until they clutter my desk. In my earlier naïveté I thought a Kanban board was nice, but work shifts around so much between started and completed.

Second problem is that colleagues who work in support and finance write important emails like timesheet cost codes and how to book rooms in an email, which I have just saved in the Outlook drafts folder since I use it often enough.

Both are not big problems, but also could be solved easily.

Here's a brief description:

The tool has four sections: a Portfolio table to track projects and their next task with deadlines, a To-dos list for miscellaneous tasks, a Wiki for saving instructions and reference notes (with full support for pasting formatted content from Outlook or Word), and a Drawer for sensitive or infrequently needed information kept out of sight until you need it.

Deadlines turn orange when overdue and green when due today. Your data is saved automatically in your browser's local storage. You can export a backup at any time and reimport it later. A Tools section links out to companion utilities that sit alongside the dashboard
