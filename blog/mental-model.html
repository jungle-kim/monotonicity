How I think about using AI at the workplace

Mar 2026

Overly influenced by management mumbo jumbo, I think of three principles (or assumptions, hypotehses, tenets) that define how I think about using AI at work.

01 — More is better

Will add diagrams later, but think of the amount of work you have to do in a week. In fact, think of a recrectangle fileld with your tasks, but sorted left to right from easy to difficult. From the left side, using AI at work can make these easy tasks go faster: writing reports, re-phrasing project descriptions in an email, editing what other colleagues wrote, etc. Now you have some extra time, ideally you would read a book, but you're somehow wired to work hard, so this time is used to extend the rectangle to the right. You tackle more difficult tasks, such as creating new work place tools, taking on projects in a new field, etc. This is in fact, the fun part. You get a sense of achievement of learning more and trying new things.

In total, using AI has made you do more, and more is better.

02 — You are propbably doing it wrong

And by it, working with AI. Especially when it comes to vibe coding, the method you have chosen may have taken 5x longer. Maybe you are not using the right tools in the vibe coding projects, or getting stuck in loops with the LLM while iterating.

Hence, working with AI is a workplace skill. It's not only about having it, but knowing how much further you can improve on it. It also means working and learning with peers, as well as getting guidance from people who know better.

03 — Make it yours

This one is the most mumbo jumbo of the three, I'll keep it as a placeholder until I think of something smarter. Best way to get started with AI at workplace is to try using it over and over. Expand what you think counts as research and experiment on how it could make it work for you. Ignore a bit of the dogma around how to improve your prompting skills and just find a way that works for you.
